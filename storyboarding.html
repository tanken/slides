And just a couple of extra ones..

Point-of-view Shot

gives an objective view of what a character is seeing

Reverse Angle

Cutting back and forth between 2 reverse angles
to maintain the flow of conversation

Rack Focus Shot

1. Close one eye. Hold up thumb and focus on it

2. Without moving, try focusing on the background

Slow

Shooting twice as many frames or more per second
then playing back at normal speed will slow onscreen time
convey intenisty during key moments

Timelapse/Fast-Motion

Opposite of slow-motion
Lesser Frames per second

Superimposition

Overlaying 2 shots such that both images can be seen at the same time
connects both images, inviting the viewers to make the connection

Animatics - Moving Storyboard

  • Helps ensure the timing is working well with the visuals and running to time
  • Checks that a particular shot has been given enough time
  • Often included is a "scratch track" - a rough voice-dub/sound effects
