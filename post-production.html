Excercise #3

My First Stopmotion Short Film

1. From Exercise #2, choose only the good photos

2. Choose your Editing Software: iMovie or Windows Movie Maker

3. Export & share on Facebook / Youtube

iMovie

iMovie

1. Go to Project Properties > Timing. Change preferences from "Ken Burns" to "Fit in Frame". (Remove default zooming effect)

2. Drag and drop all your photos into iMovie

3. Double click on the first image to bring up the inspector window. Change the duration from 4 secs to 0.125 (8 fps). Select "Apply to all stills"

4. Export the video.

Windows Movie Maker

Windows Movie Maker

1. Drag and drop all your photos into Windows Movie Maker

2. Switch from "Storyboard" mode to "Timeline". Select the first to last photo and drag in into the timeline

3. Right click to bring up options window. Under the Advanced tab, Change the "picture duration" to 0.125 (8 fps)

4. Export the video, by clicking on "publish Movie"

Copyrights

Unethical to use others' work without permission

Not justifiable to use copy of songs you bought
Why? Right is given to play songs for personal use only


Use copyrighted material responsibly

#1. Find out the owner of the work
#2. Receive his / her permission > licensing agreement
#3. Negotiate if payment is necessary
#4. Credit the owner

Fair Use

not to overrule copyright, think of it as an argument to use an IP without permission


Important 4 factors

#1. purpose must be for non-commercial (educational / school projects)
#2. must be relevant to your work
#3. copied work must be minimal
#4. must not damage or defame the value of original work

Royalty-Free

Can be used freely without paying for licensing fees
Many available libraries, some free, others with just a small fee


Public Domain

expired copyrights

Protecting Your Own Work

Creative commons, a public copyright licenses that enable
free distribution of copyrighted work


Types of Creative commons rights

Attribution (BY): may use only if they give owner
the credits in manner specified
Share-Alike (SA): may distribute under similar license to original work
Non-Commercial (NC): may use for non-commercial purposes only
No Derivative Works (ND): may use, but no changes nor build upon
